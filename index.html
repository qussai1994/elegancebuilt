The Kensington Suite
Kensington, West London

Our client requested a complete transformation of their 8m² main bathroom into a spa-inspired sanctuary. The challenge was maximising the sense of space while incorporating a freestanding bath, walk-in shower, and double vanity. We achieved this through the use of large-format Italian marble tiles laid in a book-match pattern, a custom floating vanity unit with integrated LED lighting, and a frameless glass shower enclosure. Underfloor heating was installed throughout, and the palette of warm whites and brushed gold complemented the Georgian architecture of the property.

After
The Chelsea Wet Room
Chelsea, South London

This compact 5m² bathroom in a Chelsea townhouse was entirely reimagined as a sophisticated wet room. The client's brief was dramatic and bold — they wanted their bathroom to feel like a five-star hotel. We stripped back to the shell, installed tanking throughout, and used deep charcoal slate tiles contrasted with polished chrome fittings and a rainfall shower head. Custom niches were built into the walls for product storage, maintaining completely clean lines. The result is a strikingly minimal space that feels both intimate and expansive.

The Hampstead Open Plan
Hampstead, North London

The clients desired a complete open-plan transformation of their separate kitchen and dining room into a single, flowing social space. We removed a non-structural partition wall, installed RSJ steels, and created a stunning 28m² kitchen-diner. The design centred around a 3-metre island in honed Calacatta marble with waterfall edges, complemented by handleless matte lacquer cabinetry in Deep Navy. Integrated Miele appliances, a wine cooler, and feature pendant lighting completed the look. The result dramatically increased the home's value while creating the family hub our clients had always dreamed of.

The Notting Hill Galley
Notting Hill, West London

A narrow 12m² galley kitchen was completely reinvented using clever spatial design to maximise every centimetre. Floor-to-ceiling cabinetry with push-to-open mechanisms eliminated visual clutter. A bespoke breakfast bar with waterfall quartz countertop was cantilevered from the wall, creating space for three bar stools without impeding the workflow. The clever use of mirrored splashbacks and under-cabinet LED lighting made the space feel twice its actual size. This project demonstrated how expert design can transform even the most challenging proportions.

The Islington Victorian Terrace
Islington, North London

This four-bedroom Victorian terrace had not been touched since the 1980s. The clients required a sensitive renovation that would restore the home's period character while bringing it fully into the 21st century. Working over 14 weeks, we rewired the entire property, replaced all plumbing, installed a new gas-fired underfloor heating system, and refurbished all five rooms and two bathrooms. Original cornicing was repaired and reinstated, Victorian floorboards were restored and refinished, and a new kitchen extension was added at the rear. The property was subsequently valued at 38% above its pre-renovation market price.

Before
After
The Ealing Edwardian Refurbishment
Ealing, West London

An Edwardian semi-detached property required complete refurbishment following purchase from probate. Every room was stripped to the bare shell and rebuilt to an exceptionally high standard. The ground floor was fully opened up, creating a cohesive flow from entrance hall through to the garden. A complete rewire to 18th Edition standards was undertaken, alongside a full replumb with unvented hot water cylinder. New oak flooring was laid throughout the ground floor, and bespoke fitted wardrobes installed in all four bedrooms. The project was completed in 16 weeks, on time and on budget.

The Fulham Rear Extension
Fulham, South West London

This 32m² rear extension and full-width side return transformed a cramped Victorian terrace into a spectacular open-plan kitchen, dining, and family space. The client's brief was to create a light-filled space that connected directly to the garden and could accommodate the whole family comfortably. We installed full-width bifold doors across the rear elevation, a rooflight running the entire length of the original rear wall junction, and a structural glass skylight above the dining area. Heated polished concrete floors, a bespoke kitchen, and seamless garden connection created the perfect indoor-outdoor home. Permitted development rights applied; no planning permission was required.

Before
After
The Wandsworth Loft Conversion
Wandsworth, South London

A dormer loft conversion added a stunning 45m² master suite to this Wandsworth semi-detached property, including a full walk-in dressing room and en-suite bathroom. The brief was for something that felt more boutique hotel than typical loft conversion. Working within the permitted development envelope, we created a full-width dormer with Juliet balcony to the rear, preserving the roof pitches to the front. The master bedroom features vaulted ceilings with exposed oak trusses, a bespoke dressing room with his-and-hers wardrobes, and a spa-quality en-suite with steam shower and heated floors. The addition increased the property's value by over £180,000.
